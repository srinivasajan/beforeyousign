# beforeyousign

**Democratizing legal comprehension — helping ordinary people understand contracts before they sign.**

## The Problem

Most people participate in the modern economy while being legally illiterate by necessity, not by choice. They're expected to navigate contracts written in a technical language that was never designed for them, drafted in ways that shift risk, obligations, and liability onto the weaker party while concealing those shifts behind jargon, structural opacity, and decades of path-dependent legal tradition.

Freelancers, small businesses, creators, tenants, and individuals engage in high-stakes agreements where a single clause can quietly:
- Transfer intellectual property rights
- Impose unlimited liability
- Lock them into auto-renewals
- Restrict termination rights
- Expose them to penalties they don't realize they've accepted

Hiring a lawyer for every contract is economically impossible for most people, creating a massive, unpriced, systemic asymmetry where:
- The stronger party's lawyers understand every word
- The weaker party understands almost none
- Ordinary people rely on intuition, Google searches, or misplaced trust

**The consequence:** Trillions of dollars in avoidable disputes, lost leverage, broken expectations, and compromised economic decisions.

## The Solution

**beforeyousign** aims to democratize legal comprehension by providing accessible, AI-powered contract analysis that:

1. **Translates legalese into plain language** — making contracts readable for everyone
2. **Identifies hidden risks** — flagging clauses that shift liability, restrict rights, or impose unexpected obligations
3. **Explains implications** — clarifying what you're actually agreeing to
4. **Highlights red flags** — detecting one-sided terms, unusual clauses, and industry deviations
5. **Provides context** — comparing terms to industry standards and best practices
6. **Empowers negotiation** — suggesting alternative language and negotiation points

## Vision

A world where legal comprehension is no longer a luxury reserved for those who can afford lawyers, but a fundamental right accessible to everyone participating in the economy.
